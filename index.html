About me

I am a PhD student studying urban planning at the University of Washington in Seattle. My current research focusses on how cities collect and process information. This includes privacy (how can cities avoid becoming panopticons?) as well as how to make quantitative aspects of planning more transparent, reproducible, and inclusive. Other interests include transportation planning, especially for bicyclists and pedestrians. Immediately prior to starting my PhD, I worked in the transportation modeling team at the Puget Sound Regional Council.

Before converting my long-term passion for bicycle and pedestrian advocacy into a career in urban planning, I worked on Unix/Network systems and security in the United States and New Zealand. As a holdover from this experience, I retain a love of command lines, and a distrust of analyses that are not automated and repeatable. My experience in New Zealand left me with an insatiable appetite for feijoas and a desire to someday return.
